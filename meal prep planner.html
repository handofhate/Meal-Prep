Mashed Potatoes

This should give you enough for about 12 potato trays.

ItemAmount
Potatoes7 pounds
Butter4–6 tbsp
Milk½–1 cup
SeasoningSalt, pepper, garlic powder
Steps: Peel potatoes if you want, chop into chunks, cover with salted water, boil 15–20 minutes until fork-tender, drain, add butter + milk + seasoning, then mash.

Frozen veggies can go straight into the trays on Sunday. Refrigerator meals will thaw. Frozen meals will stay frozen. Microwave later and they’ll steam/cook inside the container.

Quick Assembly Layout

Daily schedule: eat + move from freezer to fridge

This is a simple default rhythm. Adjust the number of trays you move based on whether the kids eat them that day. The idea is just to keep the next 1–2 days thawing and ready.

Day What to eat What to move Notes
Sunday Freshly prepped meals No move needed yet Put 8–10 trays in the fridge and the rest in the freezer after prep.
Monday Eat from fridge Nothing yet, unless fridge stock is getting low Good day to use the freshest rice trays first if you want.
Tuesday Eat from fridge Move 2–4 trays from freezer to fridge These become Thursday-ish meals.
Wednesday Eat from fridge Move 2–4 trays from freezer to fridge These become Friday-ish meals.
Thursday Eat fridge trays, including thawed ones Move 2–4 trays from freezer to fridge These become Saturday-ish meals.
Friday Eat fridge trays, including thawed ones Move 2–4 trays from freezer to fridge These become Sunday-ish meals if needed.
Saturday Eat whatever is thawed in the fridge Optional: only move more if you still need meals before next prep day Use this as your flex day for lunches, kids, or surprise chaos.
Microwave tip: crack the lid slightly. Fridge meals usually need about 2–3 minutes. Frozen meals usually need about 4–5 minutes, sometimes more depending on your microwave and tray size.
